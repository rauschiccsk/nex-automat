## Legal Reasoning

### Definition of Money Laundering (Article 2/1(a) Federal Decree-Law 20/2018)

Money laundering is committed by anyone who knowingly, that money originates from a criminal offense, intentionally performs one of the following acts:
- Transfer or movement of proceeds or conducting any operation with them to conceal or disguise their illegal origin

### Corporate Criminal Liability (Article 4)

A legal person is criminally liable for a criminal offense if committed in its name or for its benefit intentionally or by persons belonging to it.

### Penalties Applied

**Article 22:** Imprisonment up to 10 years and fine from 100,000 to 5,000,000 AED or one of these penalties for anyone committing acts under Article 2 paragraph 1.

**Article 23:** Fine from 500,000 to 50,000,000 AED for any legal person whose representatives, directors or agents commit in its name or for its benefit any crimes under this decree.

**Article 26:** Confiscation of money subject to criminal offense, proceeds, means, and any money owned by perpetrator equal to value if not secured.

**Article 29/1:** If foreigner convicted of imprisonment for money laundering, court must order deportation from state.

## Key Legal Issues

### Burden of Proof
Court found charges proven based on investigative reports and financial analysis. Defense documentation regarding legitimate source of funds (farm sales, agricultural business) was deemed insufficient.

### Suspicious Indicators Identified
1. High and unnatural turnover on bank accounts
2. Incoming transfers from companies outside the state
3. Outgoing transfers without justification
4. Circular money flow between accounts
5. No employees, no shipments, no actual business operations
6. Transfers to company with terrorism financing STRs

### Defense Arguments Not Accepted
- Legitimate agricultural business proceeds
- Marital dispute as reason for transfers
- Farm and livestock sales documentation
